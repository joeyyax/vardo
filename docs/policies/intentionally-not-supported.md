# What We Intentionally Don’t Support

This document defines behaviors and features that are intentionally out of scope.

These are not omissions or future roadmap items.
They are conscious decisions that protect clarity, trust, and quality.

---

## Skipping Lifecycle Steps

Projects cannot skip:
- Proposal
- Agreement
- Onboarding

Even for repeat clients.

Projects may move through these steps quickly, but never bypass them.
This prevents scope confusion and accidental starts.

---

## Clients Starting Work Themselves

Clients cannot:
- Mark onboarding complete
- Start active work
- Trigger billing or time tracking

Preparation is collaborative.
Starting work is intentional and provider-controlled.

---

## Inline Contract Editing

Contracts are not collaboratively edited in-place.

If changes are needed:
- Discussion happens in context
- A revised agreement is generated

This preserves clean records and avoids ambiguity.

---

## Global Contact Reuse

Contacts do not exist globally across clients.

Each client maintains its own contacts and roles.
This avoids permission leaks and audit confusion.

---

## Email-Driven Project Management

Email is a notification layer, not a control surface.

Clients cannot:
- Approve proposals by email
- Change scope via email
- Complete onboarding via email

Decisions happen in the workspace so they are visible and auditable.

---

## Implicit Scope Changes

Scope cannot change silently.

All changes flow through:
- New proposals
- Change orders
- Explicit agreements

Tasks alone do not redefine scope.

---

## Free or Automatic Migrations

Migration assistance is:
- Optional
- Explicitly scoped
- Typically paid

Self-service exports are always available.
Hands-on migration work is never assumed.

---

## Unlimited Customization Per Client

The system favors consistency over bespoke behavior.

While projects differ, the workflow does not.
This keeps expectations clear and reduces cognitive load.

---

## “Just This Once” Exceptions

One-off exceptions are avoided.

If something truly needs to change, the system is updated — not worked around.

---

## Summary

These boundaries exist to:
- Protect both sides
- Keep work intentional
- Prevent chaos disguised as flexibility

Constraints are a feature.
