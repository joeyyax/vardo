# Project Archiving

Projects in Scope are **archivable**, not disposable.

Archiving is how work is intentionally closed without losing history, data, or context.

---

## What Archiving Means

When a project is archived:

- It is removed from default “Active” views
- It no longer appears in selectors by default
- It remains fully intact and searchable
- All associated data is preserved

Archiving is **not** deletion.

---

## What Is Preserved

Archived projects retain:

- Time entries
- Tasks and task history
- Rates and billing rules
- Invoices and documents
- Activity logs
- Reports and historical metrics

Nothing is removed or altered.

---

## Why Projects Are Archived

Projects are archived when:

- Work is complete
- A client engagement has ended
- A feature or initiative is finished
- Work is paused indefinitely

Archiving represents **closure**, not loss.

---

## Default Visibility Rules

- Default lists show **Active projects only**
- Archived projects are hidden to reduce noise
- Archived projects can always be included intentionally

This keeps everyday workflows focused on current work.

---

## Accessing Archived Projects

Archived projects can be accessed via:

- Search (through progressive expansion)
- Filters (e.g. “Include archived”)
- Reports (explicit filters only)

Archived projects are clearly labeled and visually muted when shown.

---

## Re-activating a Project

Archived projects can be restored at any time.

Restoring a project:
- Returns it to Active state
- Makes it visible in default lists and selectors
- Preserves all existing data and history

No reconfiguration is required.

---

## What Archiving Is Not

Archiving does **not**:

- Delete data
- Hide information permanently
- Remove projects from reports
- Affect billing history
- Require administrator intervention

Archiving is reversible and safe.

---

## Design Principle

> Scope never hides or removes user data without a clear, intentional action.

Archiving is explicit, predictable, and user-controlled.

---

## Summary

Projects naturally move through a lifecycle.

Archiving allows Scope to stay calm and focused while preserving the full record of work — past and present.
