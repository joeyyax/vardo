# Internal Work

Internal work represents non-client work performed by an individual or business.

It is a core concept in Scope.

---

## Definition

Internal work includes:
- Personal projects
- Internal tools
- Operations and admin
- Marketing and content
- Research and experimentation

Internal work is real work.

---

## Why Internal Exists

Most tools hide or devalue non-billable work.

Scope does not.

Internal work:
- Consumes time
- Has real cost
- Affects capacity and planning
- Explains gaps between effort and revenue

Tracking it honestly leads to better decisions.

---

## Modeling Decision

Internal work is modeled as a **client** for structural consistency.

This allows:
- The same project and task model
- The same time tracking flow
- The same reporting logic
- Fewer special cases in the system

Internal is not a workaround.  
It is a deliberate modeling choice.

---

## Defaults

- Every organization includes an **Internal** client
- The first project defaults to Internal
- Internal cannot be deleted or disabled

Users may ignore Internal work, but it always exists.

---

## Behavior

Internal work:
- Is tracked like any other work
- Appears in reports and analytics
- Contributes to totals, trends, and utilization
- Is non-invoiced by design

Internal work does **not**:
- Generate invoices
- Appear in client portals
- Count toward external client limits

---

## Reporting

Internal work is included in reports by default.

This ensures:
- Honest time totals
- Accurate utilization
- Clear visibility into effort vs revenue

Internal work is excluded only from:
- Client-facing reports
- Invoices
- External portals

Users may:
- Filter internal work in reports
- Compare internal vs external time
- Understand effort vs revenue gaps

Hiding internal work should be a conscious choice, not the default.

---

## Design Guardrail

When designing features:
- Internal work must behave like real work
- Exceptions should be billing-related only
- Reporting should remain honest
- UX should never imply internal work is "lesser"

If a decision makes internal work harder to track, removes it from totals, or treats it as second-class — that decision should be reconsidered.

---

## What Internal Is Not

Internal work is:
- ❌ Not a demo
- ❌ Not a sandbox
- ❌ Not second-class
- ❌ Not hidden

Internal work is measured, not billed.

---

## Principle

> Internal work is non-invoiced, not invisible.
