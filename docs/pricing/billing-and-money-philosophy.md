# Billing & Money Philosophy

This document defines how billing works and the principles behind it.

The goal is transparency, predictability, and trust.

---

## Core Belief

Billing should never feel surprising.

Clients should always understand:
- When billing starts
- What they are paying for
- Why something is billable

---

## When Billing Begins

Billing begins only when:
- The agreement is accepted
- Onboarding is complete
- The project enters Active

Preparation is not billable unless explicitly agreed.

---

## Proposals Define Billing

Proposals define:
- Rates
- Retainers
- Included work
- Time windows

Work outside a proposal is not billable until explicitly approved.

---

## Retainers

Retainers:
- Are time-based, not obligation-based
- Reset on a predictable cadence
- Allow limited rollover (as defined in the agreement)

Unused time is not treated as a failure.
It reflects actual usage.

---

## No Silent Charges

The system does not support:
- Auto-billing for vague “effort”
- Background time tracking without context
- Charges without visible work or agreement

All billable work ties back to:
- A task
- A request
- Or an explicit approval

---

## Pauses and Delays

If a project is paused due to:
- Missing access
- Client-side delays
- External dependencies

Billing pauses unless otherwise agreed.

This avoids adversarial accounting.

---

## Change Orders

When scope changes:
- A new proposal or addendum is created
- Pricing is reviewed
- Work proceeds only after acceptance

This protects both sides.

---

## Invoicing

Invoices:
- Are clear and readable
- Reference the project and period
- Avoid excessive line-item noise

The goal is understanding, not justification.

---

## Data Ownership and Hosting

Clients always own their data.

Hosting fees cover:
- Infrastructure management
- Backups
- Deployments

They do not limit data access or exports.

---

## Exits and Final Billing

Ending a project or hosting:
- Does not trigger surprise fees
- Does not block data access
- Does not require justification

Final invoices reflect only agreed work.

---

## Summary

Billing exists to support the work, not complicate it.

If billing ever feels unclear, defensive, or stressful, something needs to be fixed.
