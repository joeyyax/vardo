# Billing vs Measurement

Scope separates **measurement** from **billing**.

This distinction is intentional.

---

## Measurement Is the Truth Layer

Measurement answers:
- Where did time go?
- What did work actually cost?
- How busy was I or my team?
- What work consumed effort?

Measurement must be complete and honest.

All work is measured.

---

## Billing Is a Presentation Layer

Billing answers:
- What should a client be invoiced for?
- What appears on an invoice?
- What gets sent externally?

Billing is selective by design.

Not all work is billed.

---

## Why This Matters

Conflating billing and measurement leads to:
- Hidden effort
- Skewed profitability
- Burnout masked as efficiency
- Poor planning decisions

Scope refuses to hide work just because it isn’t invoiced.

---

## Practical Implications

- Internal work appears in reports
- Internal work affects totals and trends
- Internal work informs utilization and cost
- Internal work does not generate invoices

This separation keeps insight accurate and billing clean.

---

## Summary

Measurement shows reality.  
Billing communicates selectively.

Scope treats them differently on purpose.
