# Pricing Philosophy

Pricing should feel predictable, fair, and boring — in a good way.

This product is designed to reduce stress, not create new questions.

---

## What We Believe

- You shouldn’t pay for chaos
- You shouldn’t pay for unused features
- You shouldn’t pay more just because your business is doing well
- Pricing should never surprise you

The product already does enough thinking.
Pricing shouldn’t add more.

---

## No Seat-Based Pricing

We don’t believe in charging per seat.

Small teams collaborate.
Freelancers grow slowly.
Billing shouldn’t punish that.

You pay for the system — not the headcount.

---

## Modular, Not Bloated

Features are modular by design.

Enable what you need:
- Time tracking
- Invoicing
- Expenses
- Project management
- Proposals & contracts

You shouldn’t have to buy features you don’t use.

---

## Predictable Over “Scalable”

We prefer predictable pricing over aggressive scaling.

That means:
- Flat monthly plans
- Clear limits
- No surprise overages
- No usage traps

If pricing needs to change, it should be obvious *before* it happens.

---

## Why This Matters

Client work already has enough uncertainty:
- Scope
- Timelines
- Requirements

Your tools shouldn’t add financial ambiguity on top of that.

Pricing should feel like solid ground.

---

## Long-Term View

This product is built to last.

That means:
- Sustainable pricing
- No race to the bottom
- No bait-and-switch tiers
- No pressure to “upgrade” for basic functionality

We’d rather earn long-term trust than short-term conversions.

---

## Summary

Good pricing disappears.

If you’re thinking about the product instead of the bill, it’s working.
