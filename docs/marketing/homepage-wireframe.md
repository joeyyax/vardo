# Homepage Wireframe

This document defines the structure and intent of the public homepage.

It is a layout guide, not visual design.

---

## Section 1 — Hero

**Purpose:** Immediate recognition

- Headline: clear, calm, confident
- Subhead: relief-oriented, not feature-based
- Primary CTA: “Build your workflow”
- Optional subtle product screenshot

---

## Section 2 — Problem Recognition

**Purpose:** “This understands me”

- Short list of real pains
- No blame, no shaming
- Avoid naming competitors
- Avoid features

---

## Section 3 — The Difference

**Purpose:** Reframe expectations

- Contrast flexibility vs clarity
- Introduce opinionated nature
- Establish trust

---

## Section 4 — Lifecycle

**Purpose:** Core differentiation

- Simple linear flow:
  - Proposal
  - Agreement
  - Onboarding
  - Active work
  - Ongoing / Offboarding
- Minimal copy
- This is the “aha” moment

---

## Section 5 — Outcomes

**Purpose:** Translate philosophy into value

- 3–4 short outcome-driven sections
- No feature grids
- Each framed as reduced friction or relief

---

## Section 6 — Audience Fit

**Purpose:** Self-selection

- Who it’s for
- Who it’s not (subtle, respectful)
- Reinforces opinionated stance

---

## Section 7 — Philosophy Callout

**Purpose:** Signal taste and confidence

- Short block of text
- Plain language
- Reinforces intentional constraints

---

## Section 8 — Personal Projects Note

**Purpose:** Expand perceived value

- Small callout
- Not a headline feature
- Reinforces flexibility without complexity

---

## Section 9 — Final CTA

**Purpose:** Gentle conversion

- Same CTA as hero
- No urgency language
- No pricing pressure

---

## Guiding Rule

If a section adds explanation instead of clarity, remove it.
