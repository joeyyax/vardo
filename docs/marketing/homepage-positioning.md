# Homepage Positioning

This document defines how the product is framed on the public landing page.

The goal is recognition, not persuasion.

---

## Primary Message

**Run client work without chaos.**

This product exists to make professional work feel:
- Calm
- Intentional
- Connected

It replaces fragmented tools and duct-taped workflows with a single, opinionated system.

---

## Supporting Message

**Everything connected. Nothing overwhelming.**

Time, tasks, agreements, and billing are already related.
This product simply acknowledges that — and builds around it.

---

## Tone & Voice

- Confident, not loud
- Plain language
- No hype
- No urgency tactics
- No buzzwords

The homepage should feel like the product:
clear, calm, and deliberate.

---

## Who This Is For

This product is built for:
- Freelancers
- Consultants
- Small studios
- Boutique digital agencies

Specifically:
- People who already know how to work
- People tired of fighting their tools
- People who value clarity over flexibility

---

## Who This Is Not For

This product is not for:
- Enterprises with layered management
- Teams that want infinite customization
- Client-controlled workflows
- Process-heavy organizations

This is a strength, not a limitation.

---

## Core Differentiator

Most tools optimize for flexibility and leave the mess to the user.

This product optimizes for:
- Intentional workflows
- Clear boundaries
- Visible progress
- Fewer decisions at each step

It removes ambiguity instead of adding features.

---

## Lifecycle Framing

Client work follows a natural sequence:
- Proposal
- Agreement
- Onboarding
- Active work
- Ongoing / offboarding

This system respects that reality.

Work does not start until it’s agreed.
Scope does not change without intention.
Everyone always knows where things stand.

---

## Feature Framing (Outcomes, Not Lists)

Features should always be presented as outcomes.

Examples:

**Time tracking that stays out of the way**
Manual, fast, keyboard-first entry — no timers, no clutter.

**Tasks without chaos**
A board clients can see, without giving up workflow control.

**Proposals and contracts that mean something**
Clear decisions, explicit acceptance, no ambiguity.

**Billing that just follows**
Time, scope, and expenses already connected.

Avoid feature grids or exhaustive lists.

---

## Philosophy Callout

This product is opinionated on purpose.

It chooses:
- Clarity over flexibility
- Structure over configuration
- Calm over cleverness

Because client work already has enough uncertainty.

---

## Personal Work Note (Optional)

This system works just as well for personal projects.

Client-facing features layer on when needed,
but the core workflow stands on its own.

---

## Call to Action

Calls to action should be soft and confident.

Examples:
- Build your workflow
- Try it on a real project
- See if this fits how you work

Avoid urgency or pressure.

---

## What to Avoid on the Homepage

- Buzzwords (“all-in-one”, “AI-powered”, “scale your business”)
- Competitive comparisons
- Overly detailed feature explanations
- Sales-driven language
- Complex diagrams

The homepage should invite the right people and gently filter out the rest.

---

## Summary

The homepage should answer one question:

**“Does this feel like it was built by someone who understands my work?”**

If the answer is yes, the right users will keep reading.
